# Locality

## Definition

Within the HAIN framework, locality refers to the importance of where execution happens in relation to the user, device, environment, or point of use.

## Role in the framework

Locality is a core reason edge execution matters.

It reflects the fact that latency, privacy, bandwidth, resilience, and environmental constraints can make local or near-local execution more appropriate than centralized execution alone.

## Why it matters

A cloud-only view tends to treat location as secondary. HAIN treats locality as a meaningful design condition in modern AI-native systems.

## In one line

**Locality is the design importance of executing intelligence closer to the point of use.**
