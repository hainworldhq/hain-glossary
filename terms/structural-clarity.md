# Structural Clarity

## Definition

Structural clarity is the condition in which the main layers, relationships, and roles within a framework are easy to understand and distinguish.

It is a property of good organization.

## Role in HAIN

Structural clarity is one of the central goals of the HAIN framework.

It is the reason public identity, architecture core, and toolkit direction are treated as separate layers rather than blended together.

## Why it matters

A framework can lose usefulness when its internal roles become blurred.

Structural clarity makes the system easier to explain, easier to maintain, and more resilient as it expands.

## In one line

**Structural clarity is the ease with which the main roles and relationships in a framework can be understood and distinguished.**
