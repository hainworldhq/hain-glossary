# Agentic Systems

## Definition

Agentic systems are intelligent software systems that can coordinate tools, workflows, memory, and actions across changing execution contexts.

They are not limited to direct response behavior.

## Role in the framework

In the HAIN framework, agentic systems represent a distinct dimension of modern AI-native design.

They matter because software is increasingly expected not only to generate outputs, but also to manage multi-step processes, use tools, retain context, and operate with more structured autonomy.

## In one line

**Agentic systems are intelligent systems that coordinate tools, state, and actions across workflows and environments.**
