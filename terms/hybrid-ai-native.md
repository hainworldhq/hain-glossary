# Hybrid AI Native

## Definition

Hybrid AI Native is a way of describing AI systems that are shaped by multiple execution environments and by AI as a structural design condition.

It assumes that modern intelligent systems are not adequately described through a single cloud-only model.

## Why it matters

The term exists because AI systems increasingly operate across:

- cloud coordination
- edge execution
- agentic workflows

This creates a need for a cleaner framing that reflects how intelligence is distributed, constrained, and organized across contexts.

## In one line

**Hybrid AI Native describes AI systems that are structurally shaped by cloud, edge, and agentic execution.**
