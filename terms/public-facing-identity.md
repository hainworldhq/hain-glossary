# Public-facing Identity

## Definition

Within the HAIN framework, a public-facing identity is the outward layer through which a concept becomes visible, navigable, and explainable to external audiences.

It is not only a name. It is the structured surface through which websites, repositories, social profiles, and narrative materials are connected.

## Role in the framework

A public-facing identity helps prevent a framework from appearing fragmented.

In HAIN, this role is carried by HAIN World. It provides a stable external layer that allows the broader system to be presented without collapsing architecture, tooling, and public explanation into a single undifferentiated surface.

## Why it matters

Frameworks often become harder to understand when they have internal logic but no clear public identity.

A public-facing identity creates coherence across domains, websites, repositories, and public materials.

## In one line

**A public-facing identity is the outward-facing layer that makes a framework visible, coherent, and accessible.**
